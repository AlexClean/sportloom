    <section id="post-training-routine" className="mt-10">
        <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold">Part 1: The Post-Training Wipe-Down (Do This Every Time)</h2>
        <p className="mt-2">This is the most important part of the entire process. Do this immediately after every single training session. It takes 60 seconds.</p>
        <ol className="mt-4 list-decimal pl-6 space-y-2">
            <li><strong>Wipe the Outside:</strong> Use a *dry* microfiber cloth to wipe all the sweat off the exterior of the gloves.</li>
            <li><strong>Wipe the Inside:</strong> This is key. Take your *second* cloth, dampen it slightly (with water or your mild soap solution), and wipe down the *entire* inside of the glove—fingers, palm, and cuff.</li>
            <li><strong>Air Them Out:</strong> When you get home, **DO NOT** leave them in your gym bag. Open the velcro cuff as wide as possible and set them in a well-ventilated, dry area.</li>
        </ol>

        <div className="mt-4 rounded-lg border p-4 bg-neutral-50 text-neutral-800 dark:bg-neutral-900 dark:text-neutral-200">
            <p className="text-sm"><strong>Pro-Tip:</strong> Hang your gloves. You can use a simple hook or buy a specific glove hanger. This allows air to circulate inside, which is the #1 way to prevent bacteria growth.</p>
        </div>
    </section>

    <section id="deep-clean" className="mt-10">
        <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold">Part 2: The Weekly Deep Clean</h2>
        <p className="mt-2">Once a week, or whenever your gloves start to get a bit funky, it's time for a slightly deeper clean.</p>
        
        <ol className="mt-4 list-decimal pl-6 space-y-2">
            <li><strong>Create Your Solution:</strong> Mix a few drops of mild soap (like baby shampoo) into a bowl of warm water.</li>
            <li><strong>Scrub the Interior:</strong> Dip a microfiber cloth in the solution, wring it out so it's *damp*, not *soaking wet*, and thoroughly scrub the inside lining of the glove.</li>
            <li><strong>"Rinse" the Interior:</strong> Take a clean cloth, dip it in your bowl of plain warm water, wring it out, and wipe away any soap residue from the inside.</li>
            <li>
                <strong>Condition the Exterior (For Leather Gloves):</strong> Once a month, apply a *tiny* amount of leather conditioner to the outside of leather gloves to prevent cracking.

    <section id="drying" className="mt-10">
        <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold">Part 3: The Most Important Step—Proper Drying</h2>
        <p className="mt-2">Cleaning is only half the battle. If you clean your gloves and then leave them damp, you've just given the bacteria a clean, wet home.</p>
        <ul className="mt-4 list-disc pl-6 space-y-1">
            <li><strong>DO:</strong> Open the gloves wide and place them in front of a fan or in an open, airy room.</li>
            <li><strong>DO:</strong> Stuff them with newspaper. The paper will absorb the moisture overnight.</li>
            <li><strong>DO NOT:</strong> Use a hairdryer, radiator, or direct sunlight. Intense heat will crack the leather and damage the foam padding.</li>
            <li><strong>DO NOT:</strong> Put them in a tumble dryer. This will destroy your gloves in one cycle.</li>
        </ul>

        <div className="mt-4 rounded-lg border p-4 bg-neutral-50 text-neutral-800 dark:bg-neutral-900 dark:text-neutral-200">

    <section id="mistakes" className="mt-10">
        <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold">Common Mistakes: What NOT to Do</h2>
        <ul className="mt-4 list-disc pl-6 space-y-2">
            <li>❌ <strong>Never use a washing machine.</strong> The agitation and saturation will break down the multi-layer foam padding.</li>
            <li>❌ <strong>Never soak your gloves.</strong> Submerging them in water will waterlog the padding, and it may *never* fully dry out.</li>
            <li>❌ <strong>Never use harsh chemicals.</strong> Bleach, household cleaners, or alcohol wipes will destroy the material (especially leather).</li>
            <li>❌ <strong>Never leave them in your gym bag.</strong> This is the #1 sin. Your bag is a dark, damp incubator for "glove funk."</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="deodorize" className="mt-10">
        <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold">How to Deodorize Stinky Gloves (The Quick Fix)</h2>
        <p className="mt-2">If the smell is already there, here’s how to fight back:</p>
        <ol className="mt-3 list-decimal pl-6 space-y-2">
            <li><strong>Use Glove Deodorizers:</strong> This is the easiest method. Stuff charcoal or cedar pouches into your gloves after your wipe-down. They absorb moisture and kill the smell.</li>
            <li><strong>The Baking Soda Trick:</strong> Sprinkle a small amount of baking soda inside each glove and leave it overnight. The next day, vacuum it out thoroughly.</li>
            <li><strong>Antibacterial Spray:</strong> A quick spritz with a sports-safe antibacterial spray will kill the odor-causing bacteria before they multiply.</li>
        </ol>
