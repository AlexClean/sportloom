    <section id="methods" className="mt-10">
        <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold">Top 3 Methods to Dry Gloves Fast</h2>
        
        <h3 className="mt-6 text-xl font-semibold">1. The Absorbent Paper Method (Classic & Budget-Friendly)</h3>
        <p className="mt-2">This is the most accessible way to dry gloves. While digital news has taken over, plain newsprint or heavy-duty paper towels remain incredibly effective. Crumple up absorbent, non-glossy paper and stuff it deep into the gloves.</p>
        <ul className="mt-2 list-disc pl-6 space-y-1">
            <li>The paper wicks moisture out of the lining.</li>
            <li>Change the paper every 2-3 hours for maximum speed.</li>
        </ul>

        <h3 className="mt-6 text-xl font-semibold">2. Glove Dogs / Deodorizers</h3>
        <p className="mt-2">Filled with cedar chips or silica gel, these inserts absorb moisture and neutralize odor naturally. Cedar is particularly good because it has natural anti-fungal properties.</p>

        <h3 className="mt-6 text-xl font-semibold">3. Electric Glove Dryers</h3>
        <p className="mt-2 text-neutral-700 dark:text-neutral-300">If you train more than 3 times a week, an electric boot/glove dryer is a game changer. It uses a very low, gentle airflow to dry the gloves from the inside out without overheating the materials.</p>
    </section>

    <section id="the-donts" className="mt-10">
        <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold text-red-600 dark:text-red-400">What NOT to Do</h2>
        <p className="mt-2">Mistakes during drying can be more damaging than the sweat itself:</p>
        <div className="mt-4 rounded-lg border-l-4 border-red-500 p-4 bg-red-50 dark:bg-neutral-900 text-neutral-800 dark:text-neutral-200">
            <ul className="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2">
                <li><strong>Never use a hair dryer on high heat:</strong> High heat shrinks the internal foam and causes leather to crack instantly.</li>
                <li><strong>Never leave them on a radiator:</strong> This "cooks" the padding, making it brittle and unsafe for sparring.</li>
                <li><strong>Don't leave them in direct sunlight:</strong> UV rays damage the synthetic or natural outer shell and can bleach the color.</li>
            </ul>
        </div>
