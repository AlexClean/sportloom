<section id="fight-vs-training" className="mt-10">
    <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold">Fight Gloves vs Training Gloves: Why Pros Use Different Gloves</h2>
    <p className="mt-2">This is the most important thing casual fans often miss: pros usually do <strong>not</strong> use the same gloves for everything.</p>
    <ol className="mt-4 list-decimal pl-6 space-y-2">
        <li>
            <strong>Training gloves</strong> are heavier, more protective, and designed for repeated rounds on the bag, mitts, and drills.
        </li>
        <li>
            <strong>Sparring gloves</strong> usually have softer padding and are chosen to reduce damage to training partners.
        </li>
        <li>
            <strong>Fight gloves</strong> are often lighter, tighter, and built to maximize speed, precision, and punch feedback.
        </li>
    </ol>

    <div className="mt-4 rounded-lg border p-4 bg-neutral-50 text-neutral-800 dark:bg-neutral-900 dark:text-neutral-200">

    </div>
</section>

<section id="why-pros-choose-specific-gloves" className="mt-10">
    <h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold">Why Professional Boxers Choose Certain Gloves</h2>
    <p className="mt-2">Pros do not pick gloves randomly. Most choices come down to a mix of performance, comfort, and experience.</p>
    <ul className="mt-4 list-disc pl-6 space-y-2">
        <li><strong>Hand protection:</strong> Fighters with past hand injuries often prioritize softer, more protective gloves.</li>
        <li><strong>Wrist support:</strong> Some boxers want a locked-in, stable wrist for hard punching sessions.</li>
        <li><strong>Punch feedback:</strong> Certain gloves feel more “snappy” or compact, which some fighters prefer.</li>
        <li><strong>Fit and shape:</strong> Narrow gloves, roomy gloves, and ergonomic hand compartments all feel different.</li>
        <li><strong>Sponsorship and visibility:</strong> At the elite level, branding and commercial deals can also influence what appears in fights.</li>
    </ul>
</section>
